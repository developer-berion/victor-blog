# Marion Company Blog Design System

This document defines the editorial visual system for the Marion Company blog.

## Design Intent

- Blue is the lead color.
- White and deep navy provide structure and breathing room.
- Cerulean and sky blue are used for emphasis, action, and guidance.
- Ink black is reserved for readable text and maximum contrast.

## Palette

Core brand colors:

- `White` `#FFFFFF`
- `Ink Black` `#00171F`
- `Deep Space Blue` `#003459`
- `Cerulean` `#007EA7`
- `Fresh Sky` `#00A8E8`

Semantic roles:

- `Canvas` - white or near-white background.
- `Surface` - cool pale blue panels and cards.
- `Primary` - deep space blue for actions, headings, and structural emphasis.
- `Secondary` - cerulean for links, accents, and supporting states.
- `Accent` - fresh sky for highlights, selection, and focus moments.

## Light Mode

- Backgrounds stay mostly white.
- Large surfaces can use a very soft blue tint.
- Buttons and links should use the blue family, not gray.
- Borders should be cool and light, never muddy.

## Dark Mode

- Use ink black or deep navy as the canvas.
- Surfaces should step upward in brightness with blue-tinted layers.
- Keep primary actions bright and clear.
- Avoid flat pure black blocks unless they are deliberate framing devices.

## Typography

- Headings: Space Grotesk or an equivalent geometric sans.
- Body: Inter or an equivalent neutral sans.
- Headlines may use deep space blue.
- Body copy should stay ink black in light mode and near-white in dark mode.

Recommended hierarchy:

- `H1` - feature or page headline
- `H2` - section headline
- `H3` - card headline or article subhead
- `Caption` - metadata, labels, and timestamps

## Spacing

- Base unit: `8px`
- Use `4px` for fine alignment only.
- Use `8px` and `16px` for most component rhythm.
- Use `24px` to `48px` for section separation.
- Use larger spacing only for page-level breathing room.

## Radius

- Controls: `12px`
- Cards and panels: `16px`
- Featured shells: `20px`
- Pills and badges: `9999px`

## Motion

- Keep motion subtle and functional.
- Use fade, lift, and soft scale only.
- Hover motion should never fight readability.
- Prefer fast easing for controls and slightly slower easing for cards.

## Component Rules

### Buttons

- Primary buttons are solid deep space blue.
- Secondary buttons are outlined or ghost style.
- Fresh sky is reserved for focus, supporting emphasis, and selected states.

### Cards

- Cards should feel editorial, not glossy.
- Use soft borders, restrained shadow, and generous padding.
- Blue accents can appear as top bars, halos, or small labels.

### Badges

- Badges should be pill-shaped.
- Use pale blue fills with darker blue text.
- Keep them short and scannable.

### Forms

- Labels come first, placeholders come second.
- Focus rings must be visible and blue.
- Inputs should sit on cool neutral or pale blue surfaces.

### Prose

- Editorial content should remain calm and highly readable.
- Links should be blue and clearly underlined on hover or focus.
- Callouts should use the blue family, not generic gray boxes.

## Accessibility

- Maintain WCAG AA contrast in both themes.
- Never rely on color alone to indicate status.
- All interactive elements need a visible focus state.
- Keep the blue palette strong, but do not sacrifice readability.

## System Rule

- Blue is the lead voice.
- Neutral tones are support.
- Decorative contrast should never overpower content.
