# Research Report Template

Use this template for the final briefing. Expand or compress sections depending on the story, but preserve the epistemic split between facts, interpretation, and hypotheses.

## Executive Summary
- Summarize the event, why it matters, what is confirmed, and the biggest caveat.
- Prefer 3 to 6 bullet points.

## Source Assessment
- Name the main source.
- State whether it is primary, secondary, commentary, or promotional.
- Explain the main reliability limitation in 2 to 4 sentences.

## What Happened
- Describe the event in clean prose.
- Use exact dates, versions, product names, and numbers when available.

## What Is Verified
- List only claims supported by solid evidence.
- Label each item as `[Fact]`.
- Preserve direct links in the final source list.

## What Is Interpretation
- List reasonable analytical readings that are not explicitly stated by the sources.
- Label each item as `[Inference]`.

## What Is Still Unclear
- List unresolved questions, missing evidence, contradictions, or weakly supported claims.
- Label each item as `[Unknown]`.

## Related Context and Comparable News
- Compare with prior launches, adjacent news, or earlier company behavior.
- Show what is truly new versus recycled framing.

## Hypotheses and Scenarios
For each hypothesis include:
- hypothesis statement
- why it may be true
- supporting evidence
- missing evidence
- confidence: low, medium, or high

## Business Implications
- Explain impact on companies, operators, buyers, or competitive positioning.
- Include who is helped, pressured, or largely unaffected.

## Implementation Implications
- Explain what changes for product teams, developers, data teams, operations, or procurement.
- Distinguish launch messaging from deployable reality.

## LATAM Angle
- Include only when relevant.
- Explain adoption constraints, pricing sensitivity, infrastructure realities, or regional opportunity.

## Recommended Blog Angles
For each angle include:
- working angle title
- target reader
- thesis
- why this angle matters now

## Headline Options
- Suggest 3 to 5 original headline options.
- Avoid clickbait and exaggerated certainty.

## Plain-Language Explanation
- Translate the story into language a general reader can follow.
- Keep accuracy, remove jargon, and explain why the story matters in everyday terms.

## Source List
- Group sources as `Primary` and `Secondary`.
- Include direct links and a short note about why each source mattered.
