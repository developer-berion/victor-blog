# Blog Angle Framework

Use this reference to avoid writing “one more summary of the headline.” The goal is to choose an angle with original editorial value.

## Strong Angle Families

### Practical change
- What changes in practice because of this news?
- What will teams need to do differently next week or next quarter?

### Hidden reality
- What does the announcement leave vague?
- What limitations, pricing, access, or rollout constraints matter more than the headline?

### Signal versus hype
- Is this a durable industry signal, a positioning move, or mostly noise?
- What evidence supports that reading?

### Adoption lens
- What does this mean for real-world adoption rather than demos or announcements?
- Who can implement this now, and who cannot?

### Competitive and strategic lens
- Does this shift leverage between platforms, vendors, developers, or buyers?
- Is this defensive, offensive, or narrative management?

### Regional lens
- What does this mean outside Silicon Valley?
- Is there a meaningful LATAM angle around cost, language, regulation, infrastructure, or distribution?

## Angle Quality Checks

A strong blog angle:
- says something the headline does not already say
- stays grounded in evidence
- has a clear reader
- connects the news to decisions, tradeoffs, or implementation reality
- avoids pretending every announcement is a turning point

## Angle Format

When proposing angles, use this structure:
- `Angle`: short working title
- `Reader`: who the piece is for
- `Thesis`: the core argument in one sentence
- `Support`: the strongest evidence that makes the angle credible
- `Risk`: what would make the angle too strong or misleading
